On the Phenomenology of the Unsaid

There is a peculiar delight in wandering the contours of a thought that refuses to crystallise. It is not quite an idea, nor is it a void; rather, it is the whisper between the two, like the trace of a footprint on a wind-swept beach just before the tide comes to claim it. We find ourselves speaking of things that never existed, yet insist on their presence like shadows at noon—obviously absent, but never quite gone.

Let us consider, for instance, the colour of silence. It is not white, though many might claim it so; white suggests clarity, and silence rarely offers that. Nor is it black—black speaks of ends, of completions, of answers buried. Silence is perhaps the hue of fog—ambiguous, mutable, not quite here and never fully there. In this way, the colour of silence becomes the mood of the in-between, of the overlooked, of the gently forgotten.

In much the same way, we might ponder the architecture of waiting. Waiting, after all, builds nothing and yet constructs everything. It erects invisible scaffolds of time, where thoughts climb ladders that lean only on possibility. A room filled with waiting becomes heavy with clocks that do not tick, calendars that refuse to turn, doors that open into themselves. It is a structure without form, and yet we all live within it more often than we’d like to admit.

Even language conspires in this theatre of almost-something. Words tremble on the edge of meaning, forming sentences that spiral elegantly without ever arriving. You see, there is nothing here that demands comprehension, yet everything insists on being felt. The act of reading these lines is akin to chasing smoke through a forest of mirrors—at once purposeful and entirely absurd.

And what of absurdity? It is the most honest companion to the nothing we discuss. Absurdity nods politely to meaning, but never quite bows to it. It smiles, half-drunk, at the universe and hums tunelessly through cosmic waiting rooms. It is not cynical, nor mocking—it simply is. As are we, in this moment, reflecting on reflections of reflections.

So we arrive—inevitably and without warning—at the conclusion that does not conclude. Nothing was promised, and everything was suggested. Like a dream you forgot before waking or a joke you never understood but laughed at anyway. In the end, this page has said nothing, and yet, you have read it all the same.

And that, perhaps, is something.

In Search of What Remains Human

I am sickened by the stench of this age. Every breath feels heavy with rot, the slow decay of meaning, the quiet death of sincerity beneath a mountain of noise. We worship screens like shrines, kneeling before hollow idols of fame and vanity. Our laughter is rehearsed, our empathy conditional, our dreams sold back to us in pixelated fragments.

The world parades its progress while bleeding from the soul, politicians lie in polished suits,influencers sell illusions wrapped in smiles, and the crowd applauds, too numb to care.We’ve forgotten silence, forgotten touch, forgotten truth. Even love feels transactional now, measured in likes, attention, and performance.

I look around and feel a sickness grow, a deep, unrelenting disgust for the emptiness that has become our daily ritual. Humanity has turned its heart to stone and painted it gold to hide the cracks (not to be confused with kintsugi).
